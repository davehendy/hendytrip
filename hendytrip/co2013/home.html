Day 3 (Sunday) - More Denver time

More beer I expect.
Prepare for the first day on the road tomorrow.

Day 4 (Monday) - Drive from Denver to Ouray (300 miles)

Pile in to the SUV and head off into the Rockies.
The journey takes us out of the Denver area on US285, up over Kenosha Pass at 10,001 feet. Then into the South Park basin before we encounter the Collegiate Peaks near Buena Vista.
Turn west onto US-50 near Salida, heading for Gunnison and Montrose.
Then head south on US-550 towards the San Juan Mountains and a night in Ouray (elev 7792) at Twin Peaks.
Evening cocktails can be taken at Ouray Brewery.

Day 5 (Tuesday) - Drive from Ouray to Durango (70 miles)

Climb steeply southbound out of Ouray along US-550 - the twisting and scenic Million Dollar Highway towards Silverton sitting high in the San Juan mountains at 9,305 feet.
Stop for a while at Silverton and maybe checkout the Durango & Silverton Railroad arrivals at the end of 10th St. (Trains arrive from Durango @ 1130 & 1215).
Then continue southbound on US-550 as it drops down into Durango where we'll stay for one night at the Durango Downtown Inn

Durango offers a number of good watering holes:
Steamworks Brewing Carver Brewing Lady Falconburgh's .

Day 6 (Wednesday) Durango to Springdale - Zion (424 miles)

Plenty of desert driving today. An early start will be a good idea.
Head west from Durango on US-160 through Cortez and on to Four Corners where Colorado, New Mexico, Arizona & Utah all meet.
Then continue along US-160 until we reach a right turn onto US-191 N which will takes us to a left turning onto US-163 S for the journey through Monument Valley.
Continue on US-163 until Kayenta where we rejoin westbound US-160 until a right turn on to AZ-98 towards Page.
At Page turn right on to US-89 and over Glen Canyon Dam which is on the Colorado River and forms Lake Powell.
After a few miles we reach Mt. Carmel Junction where we turn left onto UT-9 W for Springdale via Mt. Carmel Tunnel.
Our accommodation for 2 nights is at Bumbleberry Inn which is right in the centre of town.

Day 7 (Thursday) - Springdale (Zion)

Parking is limited at Zion Canyon so we should grab one of the shuttle buses outside the hotel for the short journey to the Canyon.

Day 8 (Friday) Springdale to Moab (357 miles)

Off we go again. Back to US-89 N via UT-9 and then head for a right turn on UT-12 E. Bryce Canyon is on the UT63.
Continuing along UT-12 we eventually reach UT-24 where we turn right through Capital Reef National Park. Then on to I-70 E near Green River until we reach the turning for US-191 S for Moab.
We'll have done a lot of driving by this time so 3 nights at the Moab Valley Inn will be just the job.
Right opposite the hotel is the Moab Brewery where the thirsty travellers can have their fill.

Another day in hot & sunny Moab.

Day 11 (Monday) - Moab to Estes Park (410 miles)

Plenty of interstate driving today as we head for Estes Park via Rocky Mountain National Park's Trail Ridge Road which crests the continental divide at 12,183 ft.
First though we'll leave Moab on UT-128 E as it follows the Colorado River until we emerge onto US-6 BUS and then onto I-70 E.
Now it's time to select cruise control, put the feet up, get the crisps out and follow the interstate back into the Rockies.
Eventually we'll get to exit 232 for US-40 to Granby and then on to US-34 into Rocky Mountain National Park.
On the other side of the mountain US-34 becomes US-36 which takes us right into Estes Park where we'll settle in for a couple of nights at Misty Mountain Lodge. We're booked into Cabin 15.

Day 12 (Tuesday) - Estes Park

A spot of gentle relaxation in our Estes Park cabin which is close to the centre. Maybe a mooch around the shops before getting settled into a watering hole.
The local brewpub is the Estes Park Brewery and I'm sure we'll find time to pop in for a bit.

Day 13 (Wednesday) - Estes Park to Boulder

It's less than 40 miles to Boulder so it would seem a bit of a waste to go there directly. Instead I suggest a wander down Big Thompson(!) Canyon Rd (US-34) towards Loveland & Fort Collins. Fort Collins is probably more interesting with plenty of brewpubs and other places of interest so we could opt to hang out here for a spot of lunch.
Coopersmith's
Equinox Brewing
Pateros Creek

After that we could head back down I-25 and CO-119 to Boulder where we'll check in for a last 2 nights of the trip at Quality Inn Boulder Creek.

Then it's off to Denver International Airport on I-70E.
We are on BA218 which departs at 2045. The seats are the same as the outbound (13G/J/K).
