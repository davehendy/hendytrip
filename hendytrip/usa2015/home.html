Day 1 (Saturday) - Travel from Heathrow to Seattle

British Airways 777-300ER.
Seats 26A/B/D in WTP.
Depart London Heathrow on BA49 at 1530.
Arrive Seattle 1705.
The dinner menu can be viewed here.
Planned inflight entertainment is here.

We'll take the shuttle bus to the Sea-Tac car rental facility where we'll pick up our SUV from Avis.
Avis Group F - Ford Escape or something similar.
(Other possibilities in this group: Hyundai Tuscan, Kia Sorrento, Kia Sportage, Nissan Rogue, Subaru Forrester, Subaru Outback, Toyota Rav4, Volkswagen Tiguan).

Check in at the Best Western Executive Plus Inn.

Nightcap and voodoo chicken wings at Mcmenamins just a short stroll from the hotel.
Or maybe use Brella's Sports Bar at the hotel.


Day 2 (Sunday) - Acclimatise in Seattle

Sunday in Seattle so it'll be a compulsory 3 eggs for breakfast before heading out for our only full day here.

Plenty of beer & food to be sampled.
For example:
Pike Brewing
Rock Bottom Brewery
Gordon Biersch
Pyramid Brewing
Elysian Brewing

We're staying very close to the Space Needle and Seattle Center where we can make use of the Seattle Monorail to get around.


Day 3 (Monday) - Labor Day - Drive to Anacortes (80 miles)

Pile in to the SUV and head off northwards to Anacortes.
The journey takes us north on I-5 to WA-20W.
We'll be popping in to the Boeing factory on the way. Our tour is at 1030 although we should aim to be there by 0945 apparently. If we're away from the hotel by 0900 at the latest we should be ok. The tour itself will take around 90 minutes.
Once at Anacortes we check in at the Majestic Inn in readiness for tomorrow's expedition on the high seas.
Evening cocktails and dinner can be taken at Rock Fish Grill and Brewery or maybe Brown Lantern Ale House.


Day 4 (Tuesday) - Boat trip from Anacortes

Why are we going to Anacortes? Because that is where Island Adventures are waiting to take us on a boat among the San Juan Islands with a view to doing some whale spotting. Departure is at 1100.
Here's a video that gives an idea of what to expect.


Day 5 (Wednesday) - Drive from Anacortes to Winthrop (148 miles)

Head east on the North Cascades HighwayWA-20E through North Cascades National Park towards the old wild west style town of Winthrop.
At Winthrop we'll stay for one night at the The River's Edge Resort in Winthrop. This is a 3 bedroom cabin so plenty of room to relax for the night. Refreshments are available at the Old Schoolhouse Brewery which is just down the road.
Also close by is Three Fingered Jack's Saloon, reputed to be the oldest legal saloon in the state of Washington.


Day 6 (Thursday) Winthrop to Sandpoint (338 miles)

It's a long way from the west coast across the Rockies to Yellowstone. Because of that it's off for another one night stop in Sandpoint, Idaho.
We'll follow the Methow River on WA-153S to the mighty Columbia River and then an interesting route to the Grand Coulee Dam.
Then it's north on WA-155N & WA-21N through Colville Indian Reservation to Republic for a spot of lunch and to get back on WA-20E.
Continue eastwards on WA-20 and over the Sherman Pass Scenic Byway
Our accommodation for 1 night is at La Quinta Inn which is handily placed for cocktails at Mick Duff's Brewing Company.


Day 7 (Friday) - Sandpoint to Whitefish (204 miles)

More scenery as we head out on US-95N/US-2E towards Bonners Ferry close to the Canada border where we stay with US-2E as far as Libby, located in the Kootenai National Forest.
Then onto MT-37N beside 90 mile long Lake Koocanusa. At Eureka (even closer to Canada) we head south on US-93S to Whitefish, Montana which will be our base for Glacier National Park.
Just for a change we'll spend 2 nights here at Pine Lodge.
Refreshments will be available at Great Northern Brewing.


Day 8 (Saturday) Whitefish and Glacier National Park

Pile into the car for a day touring the heights and sights of Glacier National Park. One of the reasons for all that time on the road.
Then back to Whitefish for cocktails and dinner. If not returning straight to Great Northern Brewing other options are Bulldog Saloon or Casey's or any number of other places to eat and drink.


Day 9 (Sunday) Whitefish to West Yellowstone (385 miles)

After a lengthy and relaxing 2 days in Whitefish it's time to head for West Yellowstone through even more scenery.
We'll spend a couple of nights at the Three Bear Lodge in downtown West Yellowstone just a couple of blocks from the west entrance to the National Park.
Outrageous though it may seem, there are no brewpubs in West Yellowstone.
Our motel features a restaurant and The Grizzly Lounge which might be worth a look.
It might also be worth checking out The Buffalo Bar or the Slippery Otter Pub.


Day 10 (Monday) - Yellowstone

A day for mooching around Yellowstone National Park.
Bison, bears, elk, moose and old geysers.


Day 11 (Tuesday) - West Yellowstone to Jackson Hole (154 miles)

Explore more of Yellowstone as we make our way to Jackson Hole by way of Grand Teton National Park.
In trendy Jackson Hole we'll hole up at Antler Inn for the night.
Nearby is Wyoming's oldest Brewery (or so they say anyway) Snake River Brewing.


Day 12 (Wednesday) - Jackson Hole to Boise (432 or 345 miles depending on route)

It's a long way back to the west coast so prepare for a long day on the road. An early start is certainly advised and maybe seek out breakfast en route.
Head off westwards on WY-22W over Teton Pass at 8431 feet. This becomes ID-31W as far as US-26W for Idaho Falls.
Continue on US-26W to Crater of the Moon. Then to US-20W where we have a choice.
The shorter not so pretty way continues to I-84W and quickly to Boise.
The much nicer route goes north on ID-75N through Hailey and Ketchum (where Ernest Hemingway topped himself) to the Sawtooth Range.
Then through the wilderness of Idaho on ID-21S to Boise.
We'll stay at the Modern Hotel and Bar in Boise.
Refreshments are available at 10 Barrel Brewing or Bittercreek Alehouse as well as at the hotel.


Day 13 (Thursday) - Boise

At last a day of relaxation with no driving involved.
Wander around Boise and partake of the local beverages.
Additional to the hostelries mentioned on the previous day we might also be interested in Boise Brewing.


Day 14 (Friday) - Boise to Portland (430 miles)

There's still a long way to go before we reach the west coast so we'll spend the day doing some interstate driving.
I-84W pretty much follows the route of the Oregon Trail as it heads north west to the Columbia River, past Mount Hood and into Portland.
The last hotel on this trip will be a return to Mark Spencer where I have been able to negotiate a mature person price reduction.


Day 15 (Saturday) - Portland

A day for returning to the traditional Portland activity of sampling the fine city's beer products.
Warning! A spot of insobriety is somewhat likely.
Deschutes Brewery
Rogue Brewery
McMenamins Crystal Ballroom
Rock Bottom Brewery
Full Sail Brewing

An earlyish dinner is booked for 1830 at Jakes Famous Crawfish.


Day 16 (Sunday) - Portland to Sea-Tac and depart for home (164 miles)

Well it has to come along in the end. Today is the day that we set off home. First of all we have to drive to Sea-Tac
Either just take I-5N or ponder a diversion if we fancy it. There'll be plenty of time.

We are on BA048 which departs from the South Satellite gates at 1915.
777-300ER.
Same seats as the outward journey - 26A/B/D in WTP.
Planned inflight entertainment is here.


Day 17 (Monday) - Arrive home

Arrive London Heathrow at 1230.
An afternoon snooze and then reassemble in the Royal Oak.
